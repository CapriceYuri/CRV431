Key Mechanism


Rencofilstat, a cyclophilin inhibitor. Cyclophilin inhibitors demonstrate therapeutic activities in many experimental models.

Rencofilstat has shown to reduce liver fibrosis and tumor development in experimental disease models.

Rencofilstat was found to potently inhibit all cyclophilin isoforms tested — A, B, D, and G.

- Binds to Cylophilin A, reduced inflammatory damage to the liver.

- Blocks the Action of Cylophilin B, reduction in collagen secretions and fibrotic scarring.

- Binds to Cylophilin D, decreases cell death in injured livers.

Cyclosporine A, parent compound of Rencofilstat, an immunosuppressive drug. Which can be used to prevent organ rejection after transplant in a form of oral medication.

- Voclosporin, FDA approved for adult patients with active Lupus Nephritis.
